Observability for your own life

Most people don’t need more motivation. They need observability.

In systems, if you can’t answer what changed?, you can’t improve the system. You’ll either overfit the last incident or repeat it.

Your future self can’t debug what your present self refused to log.

Artifacts that scale you

  • Weekly changelog: 5 bullets. What shipped? What broke? What you learned.
  • Decision log: what you decided, when, and why.
  • Runbooks: what to do when you’re stuck, tired, or angry.

The hidden win

Observability doesn’t just help you perform. It helps you be kinder.

When you can see the system, you stop making moral stories about variance. You can say: “the inputs changed.”

Start small

Pick one metric. Not a KPI. A signal.

  • hours of deep work
  • sleep
  • time spent in interruption mode

Measure it for four weeks. Don’t optimize yet. Just look.
