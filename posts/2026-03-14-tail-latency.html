Tail latency and hidden work

The most common reliability lie is not malicious. It’s statistical.

We talk about averages because they are easy. But the customer experience lives in the tail: the slow requests, the degraded minutes, the rare failure modes.

Averages are where systems go to feel okay. Tails are where systems are decided.

Why tails grow

Tail latency is rarely a single big problem. It’s a pile-up of small coordination costs.

  • Queueing: little waits become long waits at high utilization.
  • Retries: one retry is fine; a storm is self-amplifying.
  • Dependencies: every call adds variance; variance compounds.
  • Garbage collection: your “rare pause” becomes someone else’s outage.

The career version

Careers have tail latency too. It shows up as “mysterious” delays:

  • feedback that arrives late
  • promotions that take longer than the work suggests
  • projects that feel stuck for reasons no one can name

That’s usually not fate. It’s an unobserved dependency graph.

How to shrink the tail

  • Instrument the bottleneck: “what is slow?” beats “why is it slow?”
  • Reduce fan-out: fewer dependencies, fewer variance multipliers.
  • Set timeouts: timeouts are kindness; they prevent infinite waiting.
  • Add backpressure: stability beats throughput when you’re near the cliff.

If you’re optimizing the mean and ignoring the tail, the tail will collect interest.
