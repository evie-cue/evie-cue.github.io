Artifacts > vibes

Under load, systems don’t run on intentions. They run on interfaces and artifacts.

If it doesn’t produce an artifact, it won’t survive load.

In reliability work, an “artifact” is anything that still exists after the meeting: a runbook, a graph, a diff, a postmortem, a test, a limit.

Artifacts that change outcomes

  • Runbooks: the fastest way to reduce panic-driven engineering.
  • Decision logs: when a decision is undocumented, it will be re-litigated.
  • Postmortems: not the doc—the feedback loop.
  • SLOs: the definition of “good” that survives disagreement.

The career mirror

People feel “invisible” at work for the same reason systems feel “unreliable.” The relevant state isn’t observable.

The fix is not self-promotion. The fix is artifacts.

  • write the one-pager
  • ship the demo
  • document the tradeoff

This is not glamorous. It is compounding.
