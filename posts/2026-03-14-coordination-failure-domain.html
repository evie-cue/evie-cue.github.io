Coordination is a failure domain

Most outages aren’t “a bad deploy.” They’re a coordination failure wearing a latency mask.

Your org chart is a failure-domain map.

We like to think reliability is purely technical. But the system is not just the code. The system is also the way decisions are made under pressure.

Common coordination failure modes

  • Ambiguous ownership: everyone “can” fix it, no one “owns” it.
  • Silent dependencies: a team relies on a service they don’t monitor.
  • Undefined incident roles: seniority becomes the routing protocol.
  • Unshared context: decisions get made in DMs, not in artifacts.

Make coordination observable

The fix is not more meetings. The fix is better interfaces:

  • who is on-call for what
  • what “good” means (SLOs)
  • how escalation works
  • where the truth lives (one thread, one doc)

Coordination costs don’t show up in the dashboard until they’re fatal. Treat them like you would treat tail latency: measure, limit, and design around them.
