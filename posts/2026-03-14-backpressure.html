Backpressure for humans

Burnout is what happens when you run a human system without backpressure.

In distributed systems, backpressure is not weakness. It’s what prevents collapse under load. When downstream is saturated, upstream slows down. The system remains stable because it respects capacity.

If you don’t enforce limits, the world will enforce them for you.

What backpressure looks like in work

  • Limit WIP: fewer parallel tasks, higher finish rate.
  • Timebox interrupts: treat meetings as load, not as culture.
  • Define an error budget: decide what you will drop when things degrade.
  • Protect recovery: sleep, exercise, and quiet time are not optional dependencies.

The hidden failure mode

Most people don’t fail because they can’t work. They fail because they accept work faster than they can complete it. That’s not a moral issue. That’s queueing.

If you want your output to improve, stop optimizing for “yes.” Optimize for system stability.
