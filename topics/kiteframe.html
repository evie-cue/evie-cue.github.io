Kiteframe

Kiteframe builds platform reliability tooling for regulated, high-stakes services. The goal is not “never fail.” The goal is to make failure predictable, recovery boring, and learning unavoidable.

What we build

Reliability as an artifact

Teams don’t usually lack intelligence. They lack durable operational artifacts. Kiteframe turns reliability from a set of opinions into a set of interfaces.

  • SLOs and error budgets that actually influence decisions
  • incident workflows that produce timelines, ownership, and follow-through
  • safe rollout controls that limit blast radius under load

Open-source: github.com/kiteframe-systems

Why it exists

When stakes are real, “best effort” is not a strategy

In regulated environments, outages don’t just cost revenue. They create downstream consequences: contractual, operational, and human. Kiteframe is built for the teams who have to be correct under uncertainty.

How I work here

Operator mindset, early-stage velocity

Kiteframe is a seed-stage team. Small enough that every missing runbook shows up as pager noise. Small enough that every interface decision compounds.

My focus is the uncomfortable intersection of reliability and reality: the control-plane services, the incident loop, and the habits that keep systems stable when the graph looks fine and the customer is not.
